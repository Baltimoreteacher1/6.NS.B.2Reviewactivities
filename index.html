Key Vocabulary

dividend divisor quotient estimate standard algorithm
Meaning:

Dividend = the number being divided. Divisor = the number you divide by. Quotient = the answer. An estimate helps you choose a reasonable quotient digit before you multiply and subtract.

Annotated Worked Example

Example: \( 432 \div 6 \)
1. Divide: 6 goes into 43 7 times.
2. Multiply: \(7 \times 6 = 42\)
3. Subtract: \(43 - 42 = 1\)
4. Bring down the 2 to make 12.
5. Divide: 6 goes into 12 2 times.
Quotient: \(72\)

Common misconception:

Students sometimes forget to place a 0 in the quotient when the divisor does not go into the current place-value chunk.
